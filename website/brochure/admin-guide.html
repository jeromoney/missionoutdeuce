Administration and oversight

MissionOut separates team management from dispatch so permissions stay tighter and cleaner.

Team Admins manage one team's users and device readiness, while Super Admins retain full-system visibility and control across teams, incidents, and operational logs.

How to use it

Administration centers on readiness, scope, and auditability.

Step 1

Manage the right people

Create, invite, activate, or deactivate users for the team without deleting historical operational records.

Step 2

Keep roles scoped

Assign team-scoped permissions deliberately so dispatch access and administration access remain separate.

Step 3

Monitor device health

Review active registrations, last-seen data, and stale devices before they become a mission reliability risk.

Step 4

Review incidents and responses

Use the stored operational trail to support training, supervision, and after-action review.

Governance model

Permissions match how real organizations divide operational responsibility.

Team Admin scope

Single-team user management, team device visibility, and team operational review.

Super Admin scope

Global team creation, cross-team oversight, user or device disabling, and system-wide analytics.

Operational benefit

Scope boundaries reduce accidental overreach and make access decisions easier to defend.

Buyers evaluating MissionOut for organizational maturity should pay close attention to this separation of duties. It keeps live operations fast while preserving the governance controls command staff expects.
